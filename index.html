An ode to the Hardwood

  1. My Start in the game
  2. Early Success
  3. Crashing to reality
  4. The Breakthrough
  5. Losing the Fire
  6. Coming Home

My Start in the game

I never really thought of basketball as a game that I could play originally. It has usually been the domain of Giants. A friend wanted to play though and just like any sport it required more than one to play. Before I knew it I was playing every Thursday afternoon. It was like an invisible crack in my armour had been patched up. I now had something to call my own, but it wasn't my game alone.

Early Success

What I hadn't expected was that I was going to be playing in such a hostile environment. We walked into a gymnasium filled with absolute beasts. Each of them concerned with only one thing...victory. During my first few years I was pushed to the brink on and off the court by a group that ate, slept and breathed the game. Thanks to that unreasonable work ethic I got to be a part of the best team in the city picking up two titles in my first year of playing.

Crashing to reality

After a while I had gained some abilty. Along with it also a misplaced sense of confidence. As me and my buddy improved we started ramping up our time on court. We joined a team playing in the National league in our city. A humbling string of weekends ensued. Being handed defeats almost every weekend without fail. My limitations became abundantly clear and my place in the true basketball hierarchy of Scotland apparent.

The Breakthrough

After this humbling season we knew changes had to be made. A year older, wiser and probably most importantly taller I once again took to the hardwood. At this point I had grown not only physically but also mentally as well. The game had evolved from just pass, shoot and dribble. I was now playing a game of zones, being careful about timing as well as execution. My game had never been so polished. We improved markedly in the league and as one of the key players I led the school team to the Glasgow cup finals.

Losing the Fire

While riding this euphoric rollercoaster ride I was suddenly bucked off my seat. Every athletes worst nightmare...injury. The GP diagnosed it as Osgood Schlatters Disease. The prescribed treatment? Four months on the sidelines. During this time I learned a lot more about myself beside just how good I might be able to get at basketball; I started to socialise a bit more often; found that I had good aptitude for learning. By the time I had finished the rehab period basketball seemed like one of the least important things in my life.

Coming Home

I thought the screens in basketball were tough but real life buts up some much steeper barriers. I trundled through my adolescence doing whatever took my fancy. Slowly the invisible crack began to reappear. I didn't really notice it but it was there again. One chance weekend I met a gentleman who during conversation mentioned he also played the game. After a quick chat I had been invited to a pick up game. Being back on the hardwood after all that time was an interesting feeling to have. In part I was ashamed that my fitness and skills had atrophied so much. On the other hand though it was like I was back in my natural habitat. I could sense the movement patterns returning. The feel for the ball in your hands. The millisecond thought processes. It was like being pumped full of adrenaline. Finally I was back home.
